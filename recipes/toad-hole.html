Toad-in-the-Hole

Description

The Toad-in-the-Hole takes two breakfast staples - eggs and bread - and smashes them together for a delicious early morning or late night meal. Not to be confused with the English sausage dish, this deceptively simple sandwich can be decked out with endless toppings or served stripped down. This meal has as many names as possibilities:

  • Frog-in-the-Pond
  • Egg-in-a-Basket
  • Egg-in-the-Nest
  • Egg-in-the-Trashcan

Ingredients

  • Thick slice of bread (preferably Challah or Texas Tast)
  • 1 large egg
  • A pat of butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Choose a bread that gives a thick slice of toast, such as Texas Toast.
  2. Cut a hole in the middle of the bread (try a drinking glass for the correct size)
  3. Melt butter (or a substitute) in a frying pan over medium heat
  4. Crack a large egg into the hole
  5. Cook for about 1 minute on the first side, then flip.
  6. Cook for as long as you like, depending on how you like your yolk.
  7. That's it! Serve it up!

Recipe adapted from Julie's Eats & Treats
